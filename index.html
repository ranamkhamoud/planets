Mars


  • Fun fact: Often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance.
  • Temperature: Extremely cold, but can reach up to 70 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Resources: Iron, titanium, nickel, and precious metals.

Earth


  • Fun fact: The only known planet to support life.
  • Temperature: Varies from -88 to 58 degrees Celsius.
  • Resources: Water, oxygen, metals, and organic materials.

Venus


  • Fun fact: Sometimes called Earth's "sister planet" because of its similar size and mass.
  • Temperature: Extremely hot due to its thick atmosphere.
  • Resources: Unknown, but atmosphere contains thick clouds of sulfuric acid.

Mercury


  • Fun fact: It has no atmosphere, so it is unable to trap heat.
  • Temperature: Can be extremely hot or cold.
  • Resources: Unknown, but likely contains metals.

Jupiter


  • Fun fact: The largest planet in our solar system.
  • Temperature: Extremely cold deeper in the atmosphere.
  • Resources: Mainly hydrogen and helium.

Saturn


  • Fun fact: Known for its stunning rings made of chunks of ice and rock.
  • Temperature: Very cold, especially in the upper atmosphere.
  • Resources: Hydrogen, helium, and traces of other substances.

Uranus


  • Fun fact: Rotates on its side, making it unique in our solar system.
  • Temperature: Cold, but its core is hot.
  • Resources: Mainly made up of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Neptune


  • Fun fact: The farthest planet from the sun.
  • Temperature: Extremely cold with supersonic winds.
  • Resources: Mainly hydrogen, helium, and traces of methane.
